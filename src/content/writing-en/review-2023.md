- More happened in 2023 than I ever imagined. I know the steady rhythm and the tolerance for error of my student days are gone for good. A butterfly flapping its wings can start a tornado.
- My current belief: you have to go through some unusual things — eating, drinking, messing around all count — so that at the end of the year there is something worth savoring. Life may work the same way.
- If work squeezes life too hard, you lose the time to think and to experience. That time is not only the hours at the office; it is also the recovery time after getting home.
- Sometimes there is no need to worry too much — what is the worst that can happen? But work must be done with care and responsibility. My biggest worry now is that my professionalism in this industry is far from enough. Without the platform behind me, how would the market price me — how much value can I actually create?
- Treat interactions between people from a win-win angle.
- Have your own judgment about metrics. Care less about some, care more about others.
- Having a kitten really can change a person's state of mind.
- The economy and the job market make me feel insecure about my finances.
- I did poorly on health and social life this year. Room to improve.

Most of what I read and watched below was unplanned. All serendipity.
